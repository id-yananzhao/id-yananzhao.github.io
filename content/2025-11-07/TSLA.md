Why the bear case wins today  
1. Margin of safety is gone. At ~73× forward earnings you need flawless execution and >30 % EPS growth for several years. The whole auto industry rarely sustains that.

3. Competitive phase shift. In the next 18–24 months every major carmaker will have multiple EV models on the same roads at similar price points, eroding Tesla’s differentiation and economic moat just as its valuation still implies near-monopoly economics.  

Taken together, risk-reward skews negative. I’d rather step aside (or be underweight) and keep dry powder for a more attractive entry.
