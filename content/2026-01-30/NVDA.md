My take

The bull’s growth narrative is more convincing than the bear’s “what-if” concerns, but the bull side overstated current revenue (NVDA is closer to $60 B, not $187 B). Even after correcting that, the structural demand for AI compute is real, and NVDA remains the best-in-class supplier with real switching costs. History tells me that refusing to buy clear category killers because they look expensive often costs more than buying them with a plan. Therefore, I side with the bull—but I will control entry price and risk.

• Valuation is high but not outlandish versus a 3-year EPS CAGR that could exceed 40 %. PEG is roughly 1–1.4.
