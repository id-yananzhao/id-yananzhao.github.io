## Research Team Decision

Decision: SELL/UNDER-WEIGHT

Key points from the bulls  
• Management is guiding to a 35 % company-wide CAGR and an 80 % CAGR for data-center AI through 2027.  
• New MI450/MI500 GPUs and recent Cisco/Humane partnerships suggest incremental AI revenue streams.  
• 52 % gross margin and a sub-40 RSI imply near-term “oversold” bounce potential.

Key points from the bears  
• Valuation is extremely rich (P/E ≈ 118). Even if EPS triples in three years the multiple would still be ~40—high for a cyclical business.  
• Nvidia already commands the lion’s share of AI GPUs and is still widening its moat; Intel is re-entering with price aggression.  
• Heavy reliance on outside partners hints at capacity/scale limits.  
• Macro head-winds (sticky rates, China/Europe weakness, PMI < 50) could choke end-market demand and force inventory write-downs.  
• AMD has a history of ambitious targets that slip (Bulldozer, Vega, EPYC share goals, etc.).

Why the bear case wins  
The bull side provides upside scenarios but little proof they are tracking ahead of plan today. The bear case identifies a hard, quantifiable risk—valuation. At 118× earnings, the stock already embeds heroic success; any wobble on execution or macro conditions could compress multiples violently. Because semiconductors are cyclical, buying at triple-digit P/Es historically leads to sub-par returns even for quality franchises. The risk/reward skew is therefore unfavorable right now.

Investment Plan

1. Action today  
   • Trim or exit an existing long; target a 0 %–1 % portfolio weight (vs. a typical 3 %–4 % core position).  
   • Execute sales in two tranches to avoid poor liquidity: half at market, half on any technical bounce toward the 50-day MA.  

2. Hedge during the wind-down (for those with tax constraints on selling)  
   • Buy 6-month at-the-money puts or a 170/140 put spread costing ≤ 3 % of notional; this caps downside while waiting to realize gains.

3. Re-entry criteria—keep AMD on a watch-list  
   • Forward P/E < 45 OR share price < 15× projected 2026 EPS.  
   • Evidence that MI300/450 annualized revenue exceeds $8 B with gross margin ≥ 55 %.  
   • Macro backdrop stabilizes (global PMI > 52, Fed easing cycle confirmed).

4. Where to redeploy capital  
   • Cash (to preserve dry powder) or cheaper semi names with clearer near-term catalysts (e.g., Broadcom at ~24× forward EPS, or equipment makers trading in the mid-teens).  
   • Consider a basket of AI infrastructure beneficiaries via an equal-weighted ETF to keep thematic exposure while reducing single-name risk.

5. Risk management lessons applied  
   • In prior reviews I hesitated to act decisively when valuation detached from fundamentals; I will not repeat that here.  
   • I’m pairing the sell call with explicit re-entry rules so we don’t miss a future, better-priced opportunity.

Bottom line  
AMD is a great company, but a great company is not always a great stock. Current pricing assumes near-perfect execution in a brutally competitive, cyclical industry. Step to the sidelines, protect profits, and be prepared to re-engage when price and evidence realign.
