Alright, here’s how I see the debate after weighing both sides.

Key Bull Points  
• Eye-catching growth claims: management guidance and press releases highlight “triple-digit” YoY growth in certain segments and a contract backlog that, in theory, could push revenue north of $1 billion per quarter.  
• 80 % gross margin and almost no net debt create a balance-sheet cushion.

• AI tail-winds: investors are paying up for any company branded “AI infrastructure”.  
• Technical momentum has recently turned up ahead of the 3 Nov. earnings date, giving bulls a near-term catalyst.

Key Bear Points  
• Valuation is extreme: GAAP P/E > 500 and 15× sales leave no room for disappointment. Even if revenue doubled from here the stock would still trade richer than most SaaS peers.  
• Actual revenue growth is mid-teens (not 382 %); the “382 %” figure the bull cites refers to a narrow line-item, not the whole company. Growth has decelerated every quarter since 2021.  
• Customer concentration: U.S. and allied governments drive a large slice of revenue; a shutdown, contract delay or change in defence priorities would sting.  
• Competitive pressure is rising (Snowflake, Databricks, AWS, Microsoft Fabric, open-source LLM stacks). Palantir’s product may be great, but cheaper, easier-to-adopt alternatives are proliferating.  
• Share price already rolled over once (-20 % from Aug. highs); past AI-era darlings with comparable multiples (e.g., SNOW, DASH, UPST) saw 40-70 % drawdowns once momentum faded.

Decision – SELL (align with the bear)  
The bull case leans heavily on sensational growth figures that are either backward-looking to small sub-segments or simply inaccurate; it glosses over the plain fact that company-wide revenue growth has slowed and the valuation is priced for perfection. Competitive and macro risks are real, and even a “good” earnings print may not justify a 500+ P/E. The downside if expectations slip is far larger than any plausible near-term upside.
