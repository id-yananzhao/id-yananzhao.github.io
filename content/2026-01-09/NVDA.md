Key Bear Points  
• Competition is heating up (AMD MI300, Intel’s Gaudi) and could compress margins.  
• Gaming and crypto cycles show Nvidia can whipsaw when demand softens; inventory write-downs are a recurring risk.  
• Macro: higher rates diminish present value of future earnings; tech multiples could de-rate.  
• Technicals: RSI near 70 and a huge YTD run-up invite a shorter-term pull-back.  
• Valuation still bakes in near-perfect execution—any slip could trigger a harsh repricing.

Which side is more convincing?  
The bulls delivered concrete growth numbers, a defensible moat, and clear catalysts (AI spending) that are happening right now—not just “potential.” The bears raised valid risks, but their case leaned on generic macro headwinds and the possibility of competition rather than data showing erosion today. On balance, the growth story and moat feel more tangible than the risks, which look manageable rather than thesis-breaking.

My call: Buy (with disciplined entry).

Rationale  
1. Secular tailwinds (AI, cloud, edge computing) are stronger and longer-lasting than short-term rate worries.  
2. Competitive threats exist but will take years to show up materially; meanwhile demand is outstripping supply.  
3. Even if growth decelerates to ~30 % over the next three years, a forward P/E in the mid-30s is acceptable.  
4. Past mistakes I’ve made involved hesitating on quality compounders because “they look expensive.” Nvidia fits that pattern; I don’t want to repeat it.

Strategic Actions  
1. Position sizing  
   • Target a 5 % portfolio weight.  
   • Enter in three tranches: 50 % now, 25 % if shares pull back 10 %, 25 % if they pull back 20 %. Dollar-cost averaging cushions the RSI risk.

2. Time horizon  
   • Three-year minimum hold. Re-evaluate only if AI/datacenter revenue growth slips below 25 % YoY for two consecutive quarters or if AMD/Intel gain >10 pts share in ML inference.

3. Risk controls  
   • Put a mental stop at −30 % from current price; a break there likely means something fundamental has changed.  
   • Hedge 1 % of the notional position with QQQ puts three months out during major Fed decision weeks to buffer macro shocks.

4. Ongoing monitoring checklist  
   • Quarterly GPU/datacenter revenue growth.  
   • Supply-chain commentary (lead times, capacity).  
   • Competitive product launches (benchmarks vs. A100/H100).  
   • Fed dot plots and 10-year yield; a sustained 5 %+ 10-yr rate would warrant trimming.

5. Exit strategy  
   • Base case: hold until late-2026 or until EV/EBIT exceeds 40x on slowing growth.  
   • Upside exit: if price hits $300 before fundamentals catch up, trim 25-50 % to lock gains.

Bottom line  
Nvidia is not cheap, but the growth engine is real, the moat is deep, and the incremental upside still outweighs the plausible downside. Step in methodically rather than all at once, keep an eye on macro and competitive markers, and let the secular AI wave do the heavy lifting.
