What the bear said – the most compelling counter-arguments
• Macro: Fed funds at 5 % raises cap-ex hurdle rates; a slowdown or recession could sap demand for expensive compute.  
• Competition: Intel is reinvigorated, Nvidia dominates AI, leaving AMD potentially squeezed.  
• Valuation/technicals: 109× trailing P/E and an RSI of 72 scream “overbought,” and any miss on that $6 EPS target could trigger a sharp de-rating.  
• Geopolitical/supply-chain risk always lurks in semis, and a single TSMC-centric supply chain is a choke point.

My decision: BUY (with disciplined entry and risk controls)

Rationale
1. Forward, not trailing, valuation matters. If AMD even comes close to $6 EPS in 2026, the forward P/E falls into the low-70s; another year of 30 %+ growth pushes it toward 50. For a company that is now effectively the #2 AI compute vendor, that is reasonable.  
2. Secular tailwinds trump cyclical headwinds. AI training/inference demand is proving sticky even during slowdowns. Hyperscale, defense and enterprise AI budgets are still rising faster than GDP and interest rates.  
3. Competitive position is actually the strongest it has ever been: Intel remains at least one node behind on manufacturing, and Nvidia’s biggest constraint is supply; AMD’s MI-300 family is the default “second source” many hyperscalers want.  
4. My own past mistake has been over-weighting valuation fears in high-growth semis and missing major upside moves (Nvidia 2016-2018, ASML 2019-2021). We will not repeat that; we will own the structural winner but manage the entry price and size.

Investment plan – concrete steps

  – Buy 2 % immediately (market or just under current bid).  
  – Place a limit order for another 1.5 % 7-8 % below spot (roughly at the 50-day SMA).  
  – Add the final 1.5 % only on confirmation of the next earnings beat and a close above the post-earnings high (momentum add).  

Risk management
• Hard stop: 15 % below blended cost or a decisive break of the 100-day SMA, whichever comes first.  
• Review thesis each quarter: if EPS run-rate drifts >15 % below the street’s $6-in-2026 trajectory or gross margin slips under 50 %, reassess.  
• Hedge: keep a 3-month out-of-the-money put (~10 % OTM) on half the position during earnings weeks to cap downside from a guidance miss.

Monitoring checklist
