Where the debate tips  
The bull case is almost entirely forward-looking and requires believing that Tesla will:  
a) defend high share in a far more crowded market, and  
b) reignite margin and earnings growth fast enough to justify a P/E north of 250.

The bear case, by contrast, rests on things happening right now: falling margins, rising rates, credible competition already in showrooms, and an equity price implying flawless execution. History has taught me (and burned me) that paying peak multiples when the fundamentals are decelerating is how permanent capital losses occur—even for category leaders.

Recommendation: SELL / REDUCE (side with the bear)
