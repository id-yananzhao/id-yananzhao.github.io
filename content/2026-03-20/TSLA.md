With competition accelerating and profit margins compressing, the company has to execute almost perfectly—yet you are paying a perfection-plus premium today.  I find the bear arguments more concrete and harder to refute.

3.  My recommendation: SELL / UNDERWEIGHT  
   At ~280× earnings and ~100× FCF, Tesla offers very little margin of safety.  A modest de-rating to even 80× earnings would wipe out roughly 70 % of today’s price.  Unless you have a venture-style risk tolerance, the risk-reward skew is negative.

4.  Rationale behind the call  
   •  Valuation math is unforgiving: For the stock to justify today’s price, Tesla would need to grow net income 10-fold within a decade while recapturing double-digit margins in the face of brutal competition.  That is possible but far from probable.

6.  Lessons applied from past mistakes
